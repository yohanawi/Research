Our Story

A MOBILE BASED INNOVATION FOR SUSTAINABLE POULTRY SECTOR IN SRI LANKA

The livestock sector contributes 0.6% of Sri Lanka’s gross domestic product (GDP) and poultry accounts for 50% of livestock GDP. Contribution of the livestock sector to GDP from the agriculture sector has shown considerable growth in recent years and the potential of poultry production in particular to contribute further to Sri Lanka’s export revenue is seen as high.Currently, poultry production is the only well-established animal husbandry sector in the country, which is self-sufficient in poultry products. Broiler hatching eggs and broiler meat are being exported to the Middle East and Maldives, and the industry aims to improve the quality of its products and target the European Union market.However, though the quantity of poultry production has increased greatly, there is a need to improve the efficiency of poultry production and the quality of the products if the Sri Lankan egg and meat industry is to meet international quality standards and export revenue from the sector be realised.Poultry farming faces significant challenges affecting productivity, quality, and profitability, including manual blood spot detection, poultry diseases, meat freshness assessment, and breed identification. Traditional manual methods for breed identification are time-consuming and error-prone, especially when distinguishing similar breeds.

There are a few breeds of pure chicken breeds used for poultry production in Sri Lanka, such as Leghorn, Australorp, Brahma, Rhoad Island Red, Wyandotte, Plymouthrock and crossbreeds such as Blue PlymouthRock, Golden Comet, Lohmann and and Black Star Chciken. It is important for farmers to know the type or breed of chicken they are buying or owning. However, many farmers face difficulties when identifying the chicken breed since most chicken breeds are somewhat similar in appearance. Developing IoT devices for accurate embryo identification is essential in poultry farming. The current reliance on human verification is error-prone and time-consuming. Leveraging IoT, image processing, and machine learning, a reliable automated system for embryo detection is being developed, improving breeding management practices. Ensuring the quality of chicken meat is crucial due to its increasing consumption. Current subjective assessment methods are inefficient and prone to errors. Our application focuses on accurate and efficient meat quality detection, considering variables like issue date, batch date, duration, and freshness level, aided by image analysis. Poultry diseases like Coccidiosis and Salmonella lead to significant financial losses and affect farmers' livelihoods. Visual inspection as the primary diagnosis method is prone to human error. Poultry Lokka mobile application provides a more reliable and precise disease identification approach, enhancing poultry health and productivity in Sri Lanka.

Research Project Scope

Literature Survey

Research studies have been conducted on the identification of chicken breeds. hyperspectral imaging and chemometrics were used to identify four chicken breeds named Qingyuan, Tuxiang, Black bone, and Butter chicken. Hyperspectral data from chicken breasts (400-900 nm range) was pretreated and analyzed using three classification models: support vector machines (SVM), k-nearest neighbors (KNN), and partial least squares discriminant analysis (PLS-DA). SVM models outperformed, achieving an average accuracy of 96.25% [2]. For embryo detection Liu and Ngadi introduce a novel method for assessing the viability and early embryo development of chicken eggs. The researchers utilized near-infrared hyperspectral imaging to capture images of eggs at different developmental stages, ranging from unfertilized eggs to three-day-old embryos. They applied two analytical approaches, principal component analysis (PCA) and partial least squares regression (PLSR), to identify spectral features that could accurately predict egg fertility and early embryo development. The study's findings demonstrated that the combination of near-infrared hyperspectral imaging, PCA, and PLSR achieved an impressive prediction accuracy of up to 96.7%[12]. Research studies have been conducted on the chicken freshness level identification. Traditional freshness tests for chicken meat in the food industry are cumbersome and subjective. Researchers have employed machine learning and image analysis techniques to address these issues. They achieved a remarkable 92.62% accuracy in classifying chicken meat freshness using photos taken with smartphones and the VGG16 Convolutional Neural Network. This fast and non-destructive method greatly enhances food safety and reduces losses in the industry. Future research may explore the applicability of this approach with different CNN architectures and other meat types[25]. Mbelwa and their team introduced CNN-based deep learning models for early detection of chicken diseases using fecal images from Tanzanian poultry farms. They created a dataset, enhanced it, and developed a CNN model with specific hyperparameters. The study found that the proposed CNN model achieved a high accuracy of 0.93, with XceptionNet performing best when properly calibrated at 0.94, demonstrating effective poultry disease diagnosis.

Research Gap

Our pioneering mobile application is a groundbreaking innovation in the Sri Lankan poultry industry, offering a comprehensive suite of features designed to empower poultry farmers. While many existing appliactions like "Poultry Assistant", "My Poultry Manager" provide services for managing poultry batches and feed inventory, our application provide advanced capabilities for identifying pure and crossbreeds, assessing quality levels, diagnosing poultry diseases, and evaluating disease risk level. Furthermore, our application is complemented by an IoT device for precise egg embryo prediction, ensuring a holistic solution for poultry farmers. There is an existing mobile application designed for chicken breed identification, named as "Chicken Breed Identifier" to accurately identify poultry chicken breeds with high accuracy. But reviews for that application reveals issues with accuracy, a restricted breed database, suboptimal user experience, and continued user dissatisfaction even after updates, underscoring the demand for an improved, precise, user-friendly, and secure solution. Moreover,unlike traditional poultry disease apps that serve as information resources, our application stands out by leveraging cutting-edge technology. It diagnoses diseases using user-uploaded fecal images and provides a risk level assessment, empowering poultry farmers with real-time, actionable insights for proactive disease management. Also, there are currently no dedicated applications for chicken meat quality assessment, leaving room for potential developments in this area. Additionally, the task of identifying egg embryos remains time-consuming and labor-intensive, causing significant losses to poultry farmers, and no new mechanisms have been introduced to address this issue.This is the first mobile application in the world and Sri Lanka which has all the above mentioned features in one mobile application.

Research Problem

In Sri Lanka, the poultry industry plays a significant role in the agricultural sector, contributing 50% to the livestock GDP, despite accounting for only 0.6% of the country's overall GDP. With the industry's growing importance, there is a pressing need to enhance poultry farming practices to increase exports and improve overall profitability. Poultry farmers face several challenges, including the identification of chicken breeds, embryo detection, and assessing chicken meat quality, and disease identification. Distinguishing between pure and cross chicken breeds is vital for productivity and profitability, and the inability to do so can lead to economic losses. Small-scale farmers often struggle with embryo identification due to limited resources and expertise. Maintaining optimal incubation conditions poses additional challenges. To address these issues, the development of an IoT device for embryo detection holds great promise, offering real-time monitoring and feedback to farmers. Additionally, there is a need for a reliable method to assess the quality of preserved chicken meat, considering the lack of set standards for safe storage in Sri Lanka. A mobile application could help farmers predict the quality level of stored chicken meat, reducing food waste and ensuring food safety. Poultry diseases pose a significant challenge, with disease outbreaks being the second main concern for poultry farmers after feed costs. The lack of access to reliable poultry knowledge sources and technology adoption exacerbates the problem. To address these challenges, the research aims to develop an object detection-based model for early poultry disease detection through a mobile application, offering disease identification, risk assessment, and treatment plans.

Research Solution

This innovative solution is designed to encompass a spectrum of critical functionalities, including the discrimination of pure and cross-cattle breed types, precise embryo identification, assessment of the freshness level of whole chicken, and the evaluation of the quality level of stored chicken meat. To achieve these objectives, cutting-edge machine learning models have been harnessed with a keen emphasis on precision and accuracy. The identification of pure and cross chicken breed types is expertly executed through the utilization of the YOLOv5 machine learning model, ensuring the highest level of classification proficiency. The discernment of embryonic development within chicken eggs is carried out through the implementation of Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) architecture, assuring the utmost accuracy in this critical task. Additionally, the freshness level of whole chicken is judiciously appraised via the application of CNN architectures, while the quality level of stored chicken meat is meticulously determined through a robust machine learning approach. This process incorporates a comprehensive set of parameters, including duration, batch level, secondary level, primary level, freezing level, and olfactory attributes, all instrumental in predicting the quality level of stored chicken. Furthermore, the identification of chicken diseases and the assessment of their associated risk levels have been diligently executed through machine learning methodologies. Notably, the YOLOv8 model has been expertly employed for the detection and classification of chicken diseases, ensuring a high degree of accuracy and efficiency in disease management.

Result and Discussion

The "Pure and Cross Chicken Breed Identification" research section provides an extensive analysis of the YOLOv5 model's performance, showcasing its capabilities in object detection. With a model architecture featuring 232 layers and 7,270,791 parameters, it achieves an impressive overall precision (83.5%) and recall (81.8%), indicating versatility and effectiveness. Specific breed metrics reveal strengths and weaknesses, notably with Brahma and Leghorn classes excelling. Its computational efficiency, measured at 16.8 GFLOPs, positions it for practical applications. Mobile application results demonstrate successful breed identification, while research findings emphasize a strong demand for precise breed identification. The "Poultry Lokka" mobile app addresses this demand, offering breed confirmation and comprehensive breed information. The discussion underscores the research's primary goal, emphasizing the significance of breed selection in poultry farming profitability and the financial risks of incorrect choices. The study utilizes the YOLOv5 architecture and a custom dataset, focusing on six pure and four crossbreed chicken types, effectively detecting and classifying them in images, with potential for future enhancements through additional images. This research significantly contributes to informed decision-making in poultry farming and provides a valuable mobile application for breed confirmation and information.

In the "Detect Embryo of Chicken Eggs" research project, a meticulously developed convolutional neural network (CNN) classification model focused on improving embryo detection accuracy in chicken eggs. After a rigorous evaluation of various CNN architectures, Mobile Net emerged as the standout performer with an exceptional 99.8% accuracy rate, making it the preferred choice for training and prediction. The decision to employ Mobile Net as the primary architecture reflects the commitment to revolutionize poultry breeding practices and enhance hatchery management. A cutting-edge IoT device was designed for precise embryo detection, addressing the substantial demand for monitoring embryo growth within eggs. This innovative technology has the potential to significantly impact the poultry industry's operational efficiency and product quality. The study emphasizes the urgency of reliable embryo detection, and through meticulous dataset curation and model training, the Mobile Net algorithm is positioned as a transformative tool for the poultry sector. The project seeks research funding to facilitate widespread adoption and positive industry impact.

In the "Chicken Quality Identification" research project, the results section presents findings related to the identification of chicken quality and freshness levels. A Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) classification model, after rigorous evaluation of various CNN architectures, revealed that GoogleNet achieved remarkable accuracy of 99.8%. As a result, GoogleNet was chosen as the main architecture for image analysis. The application's user interface simplifies image submission and provides users with comprehensive information about freshness levels, including the freshness level name, accuracy percentage, and a contextual description. Additionally, the research explored methods to assess the quality level of stored chicken, with Support Vector Machine Classifier, K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) Classifier, Decision Tree Classifier, and Random Forest Classifier achieving commendable accuracy rates. The project included the development of a mobile application to cater to consumers and poultry producers, ensuring user-friendliness. Research findings highlight the successful creation of a system for evaluating both the quality and freshness of chicken products. This innovation supports poultry stakeholders in making informed decisions, enhancing product quality, and reducing waste, contributing to the overall success of the poultry industry.

In the "Poultry Disease Identification" research project, the results section highlights the impressive performance of the YOLOv8 model for detecting poultry diseases. The model achieved an average precision (AP) of 0.836 and a mean average precision (mAP) of 0.555, indicating its proficiency in identifying various poultry diseases. Class-wise performance demonstrated strong capabilities for detecting Cocci, healthy poultry, Newcastle disease (NCD), and Salmonella. The study also included the development of a mobile application with user-friendly interfaces for poultry disease identification. A baseline Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model was developed, which trained efficiently, with a minimum loss of ~0.24 and a training accuracy exceeding 92%. Validation showed competitive performance with a minimum loss of ~0.297 and accuracy exceeding 91%. The research compared the performance of YOLOv8 and the CNN model, highlighting their distinct purposes in disease identification and classification. Research findings emphasized the strong demand for an accurate disease identification system among poultry farmers and stakeholders, underscoring its potential impact on poultry health and profitability. The mobile application designed for disease identification extends its benefits to various stakeholders, from farmers to veterinarians and researchers. The research component discussed the significance of the poultry disease identification system in promoting healthier flocks, increased profitability, and advanced disease management. The discussion outlined the critical role of disease identification tools in the poultry farming industry, addressing the need for accurate identification to prevent economic losses. The integration of YOLOv8 object detection was highlighted for its precision in identifying poultry diseases, with class-wise performance showcasing its versatility. A comparison between YOLOv8 and the baseline CNN model clarified their distinct purposes in disease identification.

Research Objective

Main Objective

Our research endeavors to revolutionize the Sri Lankan poultry industry through a comprehensive mobile application with four key components: Poultry Vision, Egg Embryo Detection, MeatMate, and ChickCheck. These components aim to empower farmers, breeders, consumers, and investigators by providing valuable insights into chicken breeds, embryo detection, meat quality, and disease identification, ultimately promoting informed decisions, reducing waste, and enhancing food safety.


Pure and cross breed identification

Our innovative 'Poultry Vision' component utilizes cutting-edge technology to identify, classify, and provide in-depth insights about various chicken breeds in Sri Lanka, offering valuable information for poultry enthusiasts and farmers.


Embryo Identification

Our cutting-edge egg embryo detection technology redefines efficiency in hatcheries and poultry farming. By using image processing and machine learning, it accurately distinguishes live embryos from commercial eggs, ensuring healthier chick production, improved hatch rates, and reduced manual labor, making it a game-changer for the poultry industry.


Chicken quality identification

Our advanced technology accurately assesses the freshness and quality of chicken, benefiting both consumers and poultry producers. By ensuring that customers receive high-quality and safe chicken products, we enhance consumer satisfaction, reduce food waste, and improve inventory management for the poultry sector.


Chicken disease identification

Our cutting-edge technology accurately identifies poultry diseases from fecal images and assesses their risk levels, providing comprehensive information and tailored treatment plans for healthier flocks. Boost poultry health and disease management with our innovative solution.


Methodology

First and foremost, our system is your dependable partner in disease detection. By simply uploading fecal images and providing relevant data, you can accurately determine the presence of diseases in poultry. We don't stop at identification; we empower you with detailed information about the symptoms, prevention measures, and effective treatment plans. Not only can you identify diseases, but you can also assess the risk level associated with them. By inputting parameters such as bird type, age, flock size, housing conditions, feeding practices, vaccination protocols, biosecurity measures, and current disease status, our system can categorize the risk into three distinct levels: 'Low risk,' 'Moderate risk,' or 'High risk.' This multifaceted approach ensures that you have a comprehensive understanding of the disease and the tools to manage it effectively.
In the realm of poultry meat quality assessment, our system excels in two vital sub-objectives. First, you can effortlessly evaluate the freshness level of whole chicken by uploading an image, which undergoes meticulous scrutiny through a trained Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model. The results provided are comprehensive, including accuracy rates, freshness characteristics, recommendations, safety information, and even suggestions for delightful recipes. Our system guarantees that you make well-informed choices when it comes to poultry quality. Second, we offer a novel way to assess the quality level of stored chicken. By inputting key factors such as 'duration,' 'batch level,' 'primary level,' 'secondary level,' 'freezing level,' and 'smell level,' our system categorizes the quality into four categories: '1st Level' (indicating 'Very Good Quality'), '2nd Level' (signifying 'Good Quality'), '3rd Level' (representing 'Natural Quality'), and '4th Level' (denoting 'Poor Quality'). This intuitive and user-friendly interface empowers you with valuable insights that guide you towards informed decision-making.
Lastly, for those interested in chicken breed identification, our system provides a seamless experience. You can upload images directly from your gallery or capture a photo of a chicken using our mobile application. Our powerful YOLOv5 chicken breed detection model will instantly identify the breed type, whether it's a pure breed or a crossbreed, and provide the exact name of the chicken breed. In the event that the breed is not recognized, or if an image of another animal is uploaded, the system will notify you with a message stating, 'The image cannot be recognized.' However, if the chicken breed is identified, our application will reveal additional details about the breed, such as size, weight, color varieties, egg production rates, egg sizes, egg colors, breed purposes, behavioral characteristics, and climate tolerances. With six pure chicken breed types, including Australorp, Brahma, Wyandotte, Leghorn, Rhode Island Red, Plymouth Rock, and four crossbreed types, including Black star, Blue Plymouth Rock, Golden Comet, and Lohmann, our system offers comprehensive breed insights. Moreover, in the world of hatcheries and poultry farming, the ability to distinguish between live embryonated eggs and commercial eggs is of paramount importance. Our cutting-edge technology transcends human limitations, analyzing intricate details such as color, texture, weight, and shape that would be elusive to the naked eye. With high-resolution images and specially trained image processing algorithms, we accurately detect patterns associated with embryonic growth. When a live embryo is detected, a distinct beep resonates, signaling the potential for a future chick. In contrast, no beep is emitted for commercial eggs, clearly distinguishing them from their embryonated counterparts. The implications of this instrument are far-reaching. It ensures accurate and swift identification of live embryos, revolutionizing production processes for hatcheries and poultry farmers. This technology enhances hatchery efficiency, guarantees improved hatch rates, and significantly reduces manual inspection time and labor costs. In essence, our technology is a game-changer for hatcheries and poultry farming operations, enabling the production of healthier and more numerous chicks, while streamlining operational efficiency.

Quality and Technology used

We've harnessed state-of-the-art technologies to create a mobile application that not only enhances system efficiency but also drives cost savings. At its core, our application boasts meticulously crafted user interfaces, ensuring a seamless and effective interaction between users and the app's functionalities. The pillars of our product are user-friendliness, high-performance capabilities, and robust security measures, all designed to elevate the end-user experience. Our commitment to user simplicity is reflected in the user interfaces, which prioritize core objectives, sparing users from unnecessary complexity. We understand that software and code quality are pivotal factors in ensuring easy maintenance, which is why we've adhered to clean and consistent coding standards. We've chosen human-readable and logical code names, minimized complex conditional and nested logic, and eliminated redundant code to guarantee maintainability. Under the hood, our mobile application is powered by a stack of cutting-edge technologies. React Native takes the lead in shaping the frontend, delivering a smooth and responsive user interface while significantly reducing loading times. For the backend, we've harnessed the power of Node.js and Python, leveraging the Flask Python web framework to ensure robust performance. Our commitment to technology doesn't end there. Machine learning and image processing play pivotal roles in our application. Deep learning techniques come into play for freshness level identification and embryo identification, while support vector machine learning is instrumental in assessing the quality level of stored chicken. The identification of chicken diseases, as well as pure and cross-cattle breed types, is achieved through the application of the YoloV5 and Yolov8 algorithms. These algorithms offer real-time object detection, outpacing other methods in terms of speed and efficiency. Our dedication to quality doesn't stop at development. We conducted rigorous unit testing and integration testing to validate the product's appearance, performance, and functionality, ensuring that it lives up to our high standards.
